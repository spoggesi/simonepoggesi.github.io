About Me

I am a sensory and analytical scientist with a background in oenology, viticulture, and food engineering. My academic path—rooted in a dual bachelor’s and master’s degree in oenology—has evolved into a multidisciplinary research career spanning molecular biology, analytical chemistry, biosensor development, and sensory analysis.

Since 2022, I'm a Research Officer the Food Experience and Sensory Testing (Feast) Laboratory, Massey University. Here, I lead projects in collaboration with industry and research partners across Aotearoa-New Zealand, supporting food innovation through consumer-centric product development and sensory evaluation.

My research also explores immersive sensory environments. I am actively integrating augmented and virtual reality technologies—such as sensory immersion rooms and VR headsets—to study food perception in context-rich scenarios. These tools enhance our understanding of how consumers experience food in everyday settings.

My expertise lies at the intersection of sensory science, analytical chemistry, and food technology, with a strong focus on the integration of digital and technological tools. I am passionate about applying scientific rigour and innovative methodologies to address practical challenges in food innovation, consumer well-being, and sensory evaluation.

Curios about Feast?

Feast is a state-of-the-art sensory and consumer research facility at Massey University, dedicated to advancing food innovation through sensory science. Our lab provides a collaborative space for researchers, industry partners, and students to explore the sensory attributes of food products, enhancing consumer experiences and driving product development.
